Project Description

As part of the Coursera x Google UX Designer Professional Certificate, this was the project that I worked on throughout the course. The goal was to design an application following all the steps of the design process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test.

Timeline

I took this course from Feb 2025 - Apr 2025.

Background

The goal was to develop an application that allows customers to design and order their own flower bouquet and flexibility to pick it up at the florist.

Process

The process involved user research, competitive analysis, sitemaps, user flows, wireframing, prototyping, iterating on design, and accessibility designing.

Research & Planning

The primary users of the bouquet app ranged from teens to adults looking to pick up flowers for their family members, friends, and partners. Market reports and user reviews revealed that many people are busy with work and other commitments, and prefer ordering flowers online on an app. There seemed to be a significant and growing demand for personalized floral experiences. Users wanted to create their own bouquets and see a visual composition of their creation before placing their order.

Design & Prototyping

The design process started with paper wireframes, then moved to digital wireframes, a low-fidelity prototype, and usability studies. Usability studies with 5 individuals helped refine the low-fidelity designs into high-fidelity prototypes.

Solution

With user feedback, I added features to a mobile app to address pain points.

Easy Customization

Easy customization with steps, including the ability to go back and forth during the bouquet creation process.

Placing Order Flow

Allows users to select their preferred pickup time, easily pay, and confirm all details before finalizing the purchase.

Notification System

Keeps users informed with order updates and status.

Review and Reorder Previous Orders

Includes a dedicated section within the user profile where customers can view a history of their past orders. This feature allows users to quickly reorder the same arrangements and review previous purchases.

Challenges & Takeaways

I learned the importance of iteration. Designs are constantly improved and iterated on based on feedback and testing. Usability studies and peer feedback provide valuable insights and drive design improvements. Throughout the process, I understood that the user is the central focus in each iteration.
